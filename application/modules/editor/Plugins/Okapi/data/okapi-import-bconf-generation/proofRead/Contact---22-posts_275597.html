Translate5 is a community-driven open source with MittagQI as a company backing it.

Companies, that want to use translate5 more intense and/or engage in the community, MittagQI offers a support & development contract.

  • A contract contains usually 10 hours/month.
  • Your company can freely use its monthly hours for what ever is needed.
  • Unused hours are postponed to the next month.
  • Bugs in translate5 are automatically fixed
  • Needed hours for fixes are evenly distributed among all companies with a support & development contract

Who’s behind: Marc Mittag

Who's behind.

Project lead and founder.

Marc is project lead and founder of translate5 and CEO and owner of MittagQI, the lead development company behind translate5.

Marc is passionate about developing software together in a community. For him it is essential, that users can influence a software in any way they need to - that they actually own the code. This way they are free to do with and extend the software the way that serves them best. The software serves the user - not the user the software provider.

He created translate5 with the aim, to bring the idea of free software to the language industry: To develop a software from the language industry, for the language industry.

Back in history, Marc studied comparative and systematic religious scienes, philosophy and pedagogy in Marburg and Tübingen in Germany. During his studies he started working in web development at the Sturz Group.

After graduation, he was in charge of the websites and online magazines of the “Sturz Gruppe”, which he significantly brought to the fore in a very short time with the help of good search engine optimization. Subsequently, at transline Deutschland (a part of “Sturz Gruppe”), he began optimizing and supporting translation preparation and post-processing with the help of various developments. In 2005, he introduced Across for wholesale use and, starting in 2006, was in charge of IT of the translation division in the "area of system integration and development. From 2006 to 2009, he initiated and was in charge of the development of transAct, a software for the automation of the translation processes around Across and SDL Trados.

In 2009, Marc Mittag founded his company "MittagQI Quality Informatics", which, since then, has helped shape the development of the translation industry with innovative solutions, particularly for translation service providers. MittagQI is developing portals and interfaces for the translation industry and, in 2012, published the first version of “translate5”. Meanwhile, translate5 turned into a full-blown community-based translation tool, based on a crowdfunding initialized by Marc.

As of today translate5 is the main focus of MittagQI and Marc, yet besides that they still develop other innovative solutions for their partners in the language industry.

Aside from his job, Marc Mittag is father of three children who are very important to him. In addition, he is teaching “Yoga for business” against the backdrop of his own daily yoga and meditation practice of 23 years.

Continuous cycle of development and improvement
2. Continuous cycle of development and improvement

The translate5 mission is realised via a continuous cycle of development and improvement. It describes the translate5 business model which consists of the following four process elements:

1. Development

Translate5 is developed by the collaboration of professionals. We come together as translation experts, language researchers and translation software developers who are aware of the special needs of a translation tool. Communicating and discussing the expectations with each other let to the realisation of their special needs and become true as cloud-based TMS. This process is led and organised by MittagQI as the lead development company doing large parts of the development work behind translate5. MittagQI coordinates the road-map with the community and ensures good architecture and a high code quality.

2. Using
The stable and user-friendly cloud-based translation software product translate5 can be integrated and used by different target groups like language service providers, companies, freelancers and state institutions.
3. Extending
Joining the community and enhancing the strength of translate5 is meant by extending. This can happen in different ways like:
  • Spread the idea of an open source and cloud-based TMS
  • Give feedback to our developers
  • Fund features
  • Contribute to documentation
  • Develop plug-in’s
  • Contribute to core development
  • Permanent funding
The aim of translate5 is to provide to all users the control of their own relevant software development. Therefore, all users are allowed to extend translate5 according to their special needs. This opportunity is given because open source is the conceptual base of translate5 and the flexible, extendable architecture of the system.
4. Returning
The whole philosophy of translate5 means not only that you can use and extend the software tool for own usage but also to give new versions and features back to the community and into translate5 as core product. That philosophy is based on both the AGPLv3 as well as the GPL3 license model and the idea of freedom of software.
